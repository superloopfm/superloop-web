# STRATEGIC RESULTS: Terms of Service & Giveaway Legalities
**Date**: 2026-03-11
**Role**: Expert Startup Product Strategist & Digital Legal Counsel

---

## TL;DR — What You Need in the Footer

You have a **sweepstakes** (chance to win a prize, no purchase required, in exchange for email entry). This triggers specific US federal and state laws, plus privacy regulations. Here is exactly what must appear in or linked from your footer.

---

## Required Footer Links (Minimum Viable Legal)

| Link Label | What It Must Cover |
|---|---|
| **Terms & Conditions** | Full sweepstakes rules, eligibility, odds, prize details |
| **Privacy Policy** | Email data collection, storage, use, third parties, opt-out |
| **Sweepstakes Official Rules** | Legally required standalone rules document |
| **No Purchase Necessary** | Must appear inline near the entry point (not just linked) |

---

## 1. No Purchase Necessary Statement (Inline — Not Just a Link)

This phrase **must appear visibly** near the Vending Machine entry CTA — not buried in a link. It is a federal requirement under the Postal Lottery Statute (18 U.S.C. § 1302).

**Suggested footer micro-copy:**
> "No purchase necessary to enter or win. A purchase will not improve your chances of winning."

---

## 2. Official Sweepstakes Rules (Standalone Linked Page)

This is the most critical legal document. It must include:

- **Sponsor name and address** — your company's full legal name and address
- **Eligibility** — age (must be 18+), geographic restrictions (US only, or specify), void where prohibited
- **Entry period** — exact start and end dates/times with timezone
- **How to enter** — the email signup mechanism described plainly
- **Odds of winning** — must state odds for each prize tier:
  - 70% — Free digital soundpack
  - 25% — VIP Remix Week Pass
  - 5% — Rare physical vinyl record (limited edition of 100)
- **Prize descriptions & ARV** — Approximate Retail Value of each prize (soundpack, pass, vinyl)
- **Prize limitations** — "no cash equivalent," non-transferable, limited to 100 vinyl records
- **Winner selection** — describe the weighted random draw mechanism
- **Winner notification** — how and when winners are notified
- **Claiming prizes** — deadline to claim, what happens if unclaimed
- **Shipping for physical prize** — who pays, delivery timeline for vinyl record
- **Taxes** — winner is responsible for any applicable taxes
- **Publicity release** — whether winners consent to name/likeness use (make opt-in if possible)
- **Dispute resolution** — governing law (specify your state), jurisdiction
- **Sponsor's right to modify or cancel** — force majeure clause

---

## 3. Privacy Policy (Linked Page)

Because you are collecting emails, you need GDPR-adjacent compliance (if any EU users) and CAN-SPAM / CCPA compliance (US).

Must cover:

- **What data you collect** — email address, entry timestamp, IP address (if logged)
- **How you use it** — marketing emails, prize notification, Remix Week communications
- **Third-party sharing** — email service provider (Mailchimp, Klaviyo, etc.), no selling of data
- **Retention** — how long you keep the email (e.g., until unsubscribe or end of promotion)
- **User rights** — right to opt out, right to delete (CCPA for California users)
- **Cookies** — if your landing page uses any tracking pixels or analytics
- **Contact** — email address for privacy inquiries

---

## 4. Terms & Conditions (General Site T&C — Linked Page)

Covers the general website. Can be lighter-weight than the sweepstakes rules but must include:

- Intellectual property ownership of soundpacks and content
- Limitation of liability
- No warranties
- Governing law

---

## 5. State-Specific Warnings

🔴 **New York and Florida** require sweepstakes operators to **register and post a bond** if the total prize value (ARV) exceeds **$5,000**. Calculate your total prize ARV:
- 100 vinyl records × (retail value each)
- All VIP passes × (retail value each)
- All soundpacks × (retail value each)

If total ARV exceeds $5,000, you need a NY/FL attorney before launch.

🟡 **Rhode Island** requires registration over $500 ARV.

🔵 For a startup soft-launch with sub-$5,000 total ARV, most states are fine with just posting Official Rules.
